Our Heritage

Five Centuries of Healing

From a humble clinic in Ming Dynasty China to Orchard Road, Singapore — the Yuan Tai Tang tradition endures.

In the fifteenth year of Emperor Zhengde's reign (1520), in the ancient town of Zhouzhuang, a practitioner named Yuan Jiaji established a humble clinic. He called it "Yuan Tai Tang" — Yuan's Hall of Great Peace.

Yuan Jiaji was not merely a physician. He was a guardian of tradition — a man who believed medicine was not a trade, but a calling. He treated the poor without payment and never turned a patient away.

Under the reign of Emperor Qianlong, the clinic's reputation flourished. Through five centuries, the Yuan family refined their art — never chasing fame, always pursuing truth in healing.

Now, Yuan Jinsheng — the latest inheritor — has brought Yuan Tai Tang to Singapore. The methods are ancient. The clinic is modern. The principle is unchanged.
